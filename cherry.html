The Cherry Tree

Few trees can rival the cherry tree (Prunus spp.) for the beauty of its spring blossoms. The cherry tree is the most widely planted ornamental tree in the United States. This genus contains over 400 species and many hybrids and cultivars native to temperate zones in Asia, Europe and America. Cherry trees require cold weather to flower and fruit, so they do not grow in tropical climates and would not grow well indoors.
